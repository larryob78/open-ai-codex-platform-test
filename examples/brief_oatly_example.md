## Research Findings

### 1. Market Landscape

The UK plant-based milk market is valued at approximately £560M (2025), growing at 7% CAGR. Oat milk has overtaken soy and almond to become the #1 plant-based milk in the UK, commanding ~65% of the barista/coffee segment. However, plant-based milk still represents only ~10% of total UK milk consumption, meaning 90% of coffee drinkers have never switched. The independent coffee shop sector comprises ~10,000+ outlets across the UK and acts as a cultural tastemaker for mainstream adoption.

### 2. Competitor Insights

- **Minor Figures** — Strong indie coffee shop presence, "specialty coffee" positioning, cool brand identity
- **Califia Farms** — US import, premium almond-focused, growing UK presence
- **Alpro (Danone)** — Mass-market leader, available everywhere, but perceived as "supermarket" not "specialty"
- **Own-label** — Tesco, Sainsbury's etc. growing rapidly on price

Oatly's advantage: strongest brand recognition and barista-endorsed credibility. Weakness: perceived as "already everywhere" — losing the challenger brand energy that made it famous.

### 3. Audience Demographics & Psychographics

**The Reluctant Switcher (aged 25-45, urban UK)**
- Drinks 2-3 coffees daily, mostly from independent coffee shops
- Has *heard of* oat milk but never ordered it — defaults to semi-skimmed
- Not anti-plant-based, just hasn't had a reason to change
- Values taste above all — will not sacrifice their coffee ritual for ethics
- Influenced by barista recommendations and what they see others ordering
- Scrolls Instagram/TikTok, listens to podcasts, reads TimeOut/Eater

### 4. Cultural & Category Tensions

- **Tension 1:** People want to be seen as progressive but don't want to sacrifice taste. Dairy feels "safe."
- **Tension 2:** Oat milk is now mainstream — some view it as a trend that peaked. The novelty has worn off for non-adopters.
- **Tension 3:** The independent coffee shop is a space of self-expression. What you order signals who you are — and ordering "normal milk" is the safe default.
- **Category convention to challenge:** Plant-based milk marketing talks about sustainability and ethics. Non-adopters don't care — they care about taste.

### 5. Trends

- "Quiet sustainability" — people making low-effort green choices without broadcasting them
- Barista influence growing — TikTok coffee culture, latte art, specialty drinks
- Taste-first messaging outperforming ethics-first messaging in food/bev (Kantar 2025 data)

## Strategic Direction

### 1. Why This Brief Exists

Oatly has won the converted. The next wave of growth requires converting the 90% of UK coffee drinkers who have never tried oat milk — not because they oppose it, but because they've never had a reason to. This is a trial-driving brief.

### 2. Campaign Objective

Drive 500,000 first-time oat milk trials in UK independent coffee shops within 6 months of launch.

### 3. Target Audience

**"Creature of Habit Chris"** — 28-42, urban professional, gender-neutral
- Orders the same flat white with semi-skimmed every single morning
- Knows oat milk exists but has literally never considered ordering it
- Not ideological about dairy — it's just... what they've always had
- Cares deeply about their coffee tasting right. The morning coffee is sacred.
- Would switch if they *knew* it tasted as good or better — but can't be bothered to find out
- Trusts their barista more than any ad

### 4. Insight

**"You've never tried it because you've never been given a reason to. Not because you tried it and didn't like it."**

The biggest barrier to oat milk isn't rejection — it's indifference. Most people haven't actively said no. They've just never said yes.

### 5. Single-Minded Proposition

**You've never actually said no to oat milk. You just haven't said yes yet.**

### 6. Reasons to Believe

1. In blind taste tests, 7 out of 10 coffee drinkers can't tell the difference between Oatly Barista and semi-skimmed in a flat white (Oatly internal research)
2. 85% of people who try oat milk in coffee re-order it within 30 days (barista survey data)
3. Oatly Barista Edition is the #1 recommended milk alternative by UK baristas

### 7. Desired Response

- **Think:** "Wait, I've never actually tried it. Maybe I should."
- **Feel:** Curious. Slightly challenged. Amused that they've been on autopilot.
- **Do:** Order oat milk next time they're at their regular coffee shop.

### 8. Success Metrics

1. 500K first-time oat milk trials tracked via partner coffee shop POS data
2. 15% uplift in Oatly Barista Edition volume sales in independent coffee shops
3. 30% increase in "oat milk" search interest (Google Trends, UK)
4. Earned media value target: £5M
5. Social engagement: 50M impressions, 2M+ engagements

## Creative Direction

### 1. Tone & Voice

Oatly's trademark irreverent, self-aware, slightly awkward tone — but warmer and more inviting than confrontational. We're not lecturing anyone about dairy. We're gently pointing out something they've never noticed about their own behaviour. Think: a friend who's amused, not judgmental.

### 2. Visual Direction

- **Aesthetic:** Oatly's iconic hand-drawn illustration style, but applied to coffee shop environments — chalk on blackboard, handwritten napkin notes, window decals
- **Colour palette:** Oatly blue + coffee browns + chalk white
- **Imagery:** Close-up coffee moments — the pour, the first sip, the latte art — but with a visual "glitch" or question mark that makes you pause
- **Distinctive brand asset to build:** "The Question Mark Latte" — latte art in the shape of a question mark, appearing across all touchpoints

### 3. Tagline Options

1. **"You've never said no. Just say yes."**
2. **"Have you actually tried it though?"**
3. **"Your flat white doesn't know the difference."**
4. **"Still haven't tried it? Weird."**
5. **"The milk you've been ignoring tastes like the milk you haven't."**

### 4. The Big Idea

**"The Milk You've Never Said No To"**

We reframe oat milk from something people have *rejected* to something they've simply *never tried*. The entire campaign is built around this single cognitive reframe. Every execution asks one question in a hundred different ways: "Have you actually tried it, though?"

In coffee shops, we place a simple question on every table, every cup sleeve, every napkin: *"Still haven't tried it?"* We turn the barista into our media channel — they simply ask "want to try oat milk today?" when the customer orders. We make saying "yes" effortless and saying "no" feel weirdly deliberate.

This mirrors the mechanic that made **Burger King's Whopper Detour** work: turn the competitor's biggest advantage (dairy's incumbency) into a vulnerability (you've never even questioned it).

### 5. Content Formats & Channels

| Format | Channel | Rationale |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| In-shop table talkers, cup sleeves, napkins | 2,000+ independent coffee shops | Point of decision — lowest friction to trial |
| Barista script cards | Coffee shop partners | Most trusted recommender |
| 15s social video — "The Question" series | TikTok, Instagram Reels | Each video shows someone being asked if they've tried it and realising they haven't |
| OOH posters near coffee shops | London, Manchester, Bristol, Edinburgh | Proximity targeting to drive in-store |
| Podcast mid-rolls | Coffee/culture podcasts | Audio format for commute-to-coffee-shop moment |
| PR stunt: "The Oatly Question Mark Café" | Pop-up in Shoreditch | A café that only serves question marks — order any coffee and the default milk is oat. You have to specifically ask for dairy. |

### 6. Brand Guidelines Notes

- Never criticise dairy directly — the tone is curiosity, not confrontation
- Never use the word "vegan" — this is a taste campaign, not an identity campaign
- Maintain Oatly's hand-drawn illustration style across all touchpoints
- The Question Mark Latte is the hero visual — use it consistently
