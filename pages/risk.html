EU AI Act Risk Classification

Understand how the EU AI Act categorises AI systems by risk level and what obligations apply to each tier.

Risk Pyramid Overview

Hover each level for details
Unacceptable Risk
PROHIBITED
Social scoring by governments, real-time biometric ID in public spaces (with exceptions), manipulative AI exploiting vulnerabilities.
High Risk
STRICTLY REGULATED
AI in HR, credit scoring, education, law enforcement, critical infrastructure, migration. Must comply with Arts. 9-15.
Limited Risk
TRANSPARENCY OBLIGATIONS
Chatbots, emotion recognition, deepfakes. Users must be informed they are interacting with AI or viewing AI-generated content.
Minimal Risk
NO SPECIFIC OBLIGATIONS
Spam filters, AI-enabled games, inventory management. May voluntarily adopt codes of conduct.

Social Scoring

Government use of AI for social scoring based on social behaviour or personal characteristics.

Manipulative AI

AI that deploys subliminal or manipulative techniques to distort behaviour, causing harm.

Real-time Biometric ID

Real-time remote biometric identification in publicly accessible spaces for law enforcement (limited exceptions).

Exploitation of Vulnerabilities

AI exploiting age, disability, or socio-economic vulnerabilities to materially distort behaviour.

High-risk AI systems are listed in Annex III of the EU AI Act. These carry the most extensive compliance obligations.

Category Examples Key Articles
Biometrics Remote biometric identification (non real-time), emotion recognition in workplace Arts. 9-15
Critical Infrastructure AI managing road traffic, water, gas, electricity supply Arts. 9-15
Education AI determining access to education, evaluating learning outcomes Arts. 9-15
Employment & HR CV screening, interview evaluation, performance monitoring, promotion decisions Arts. 9-15
Essential Services Credit scoring, insurance pricing, emergency services dispatch Arts. 9-15
Law Enforcement Polygraph tools, evidence reliability assessment, crime prediction Arts. 9-15
Migration & Border Automated visa processing, risk assessment of travellers Arts. 9-15
Justice & Democracy AI assisting judicial decisions, influencing election outcomes Arts. 9-15
Transparency is the key obligation Users must be clearly informed when they are interacting with an AI system or viewing AI-generated content.
Chatbots

Must inform users they are interacting with an AI

Emotion Recognition

Must disclose use of emotion or biometric categorisation

Deepfakes / Synthetic Media

Must label AI-generated or manipulated content

No mandatory obligations Minimal-risk AI systems can be developed and used freely. Voluntary codes of conduct are encouraged.

Examples of minimal-risk AI systems include:

Spam Filters Inventory Management AI-enabled Games Search Optimisation Recommendation Engines Spell Checkers Manufacturing QA Predictive Maintenance
