EU AI Act Documentation

Reference materials, key articles, and compliance guidance for the EU Artificial Intelligence Act (Regulation 2024/1689).

Key Articles Reference

Article 5 — Prohibited AI Practices

Defines AI practices that are banned outright: social scoring, manipulative AI, exploitation of vulnerabilities, and real-time biometric identification (with limited exceptions). Violation carries fines up to EUR 35 million or 7% of global turnover.

Unacceptable
Article 6 — Classification Rules for High-Risk AI

Sets out the criteria for classifying AI systems as high-risk, referencing Annex I (EU harmonisation legislation) and Annex III (standalone high-risk areas).

High Risk
Article 9 — Risk Management System

Requires high-risk AI providers to establish, implement, document and maintain a continuous risk management system throughout the AI system lifecycle. Must identify foreseeable risks, adopt mitigation measures, and test for residual risk.

High Risk
Article 10 — Data and Data Governance

Training, validation and testing data shall meet quality criteria. Requires examination for biases, appropriate statistical properties, and representativeness. Data governance practices must be documented.

High Risk
Article 11 — Technical Documentation

Technical documentation must be drawn up before market placement or deployment. Must include system description, design specifications, development methodology, validation/testing procedures, and risk management measures.

High Risk
Article 12 — Record-Keeping (Logging)

High-risk systems must allow automatic recording of events (logs) throughout their lifetime. Logs must enable traceability and be retained for a period appropriate to the intended purpose.

High Risk
Article 13 — Transparency and Information to Deployers

High-risk systems must be designed to operate with sufficient transparency for deployers to interpret output and use it appropriately. Instructions for use must include characteristics, capabilities, limitations, and performance metrics.

High Risk
Article 14 — Human Oversight

High-risk AI must be designed for effective human oversight. Oversight measures must enable humans to fully understand the system, monitor operation, intervene or interrupt, and be aware of automation bias risks.

High Risk
Article 15 — Accuracy, Robustness and Cybersecurity

High-risk AI must achieve appropriate levels of accuracy, robustness, and cybersecurity. Must be resilient to errors, faults, inconsistencies, and adversarial manipulation attempts.

High Risk
Article 50 — Transparency Obligations for Certain AI Systems

Providers of AI systems that interact with natural persons (chatbots), generate synthetic content (deepfakes), or use emotion recognition/biometric categorisation must ensure transparency. Users must be informed they are interacting with AI.

Limited Risk
Article 51 — Registration in EU Database

Providers of high-risk AI systems must register in the EU database before placing on the market or putting into service. Deployers who are public authorities must also register.

High Risk

Frequently Asked Questions

When does the EU AI Act take effect?

The EU AI Act entered into force on 1 August 2024. Prohibited practices (Art. 5) apply from 2 February 2025. GPAI obligations apply from 2 August 2025. High-risk system obligations apply from 2 August 2026. Existing systems in Annex I have until 2 August 2027.

Who needs to comply?

The Act applies to providers (developers), deployers (users), importers, and distributors of AI systems placed on the EU market or whose output is used in the EU, regardless of where they are established.

What are the penalties for non-compliance?

Fines range from EUR 7.5 million (or 1% of global turnover) for minor infringements to EUR 35 million (or 7% of turnover) for prohibited practices violations. SMEs have proportionate caps.

How do I determine if my AI system is high-risk?

Check Art. 6 and Annex III. If your system operates in areas like employment, credit scoring, education, law enforcement, migration, critical infrastructure, or biometrics, it is likely high-risk. Use our Compliance Assessment Wizard for guided classification.

What is a conformity assessment?

Under Art. 43, high-risk AI systems must undergo conformity assessment before market placement. Most systems can use internal conformity assessment (self-assessment). Biometric systems used in law enforcement require third-party assessment by a notified body.

Does the Act apply to AI systems developed outside the EU?

Yes. The Act has extraterritorial reach. If an AI system is placed on the EU market or its output is used within the EU, the Act applies regardless of where the provider or deployer is established.
