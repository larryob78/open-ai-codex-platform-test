Section 02
The Problem It Solves
The EU AI Act creates significant compliance challenges for SMEs who lack the legal teams, technical resources, and regulatory expertise that large enterprises can afford.

The Challenge for SMEs

  • Regulatory complexity — The EU AI Act spans 85+ articles, 13 annexes, and cross-references GDPR, the Machinery Regulation, and sectoral laws. SMEs cannot afford dedicated legal teams to interpret it all.
  • Risk classification confusion — Determining whether an AI system is "high-risk" requires mapping it against Annex III categories, understanding intended purpose, and evaluating safety implications. Most SMEs guess wrong.
  • Compliance deadlines are imminent — Prohibited practices were banned Feb 2025, GPAI rules started Aug 2025, and full high-risk compliance is required by Aug 2, 2026. Many SMEs haven't started.
  • No affordable tooling exists — Enterprise GRC platforms (OneTrust, ServiceNow) cost $50k-$500k/year. SMEs with 2-50 AI systems need something purpose-built and affordable.
  • Audit evidence gaps — Even compliant organisations struggle to produce the documentation, test results, and traceability records that notified bodies and market surveillance authorities require.
  • Penalties are severe — Fines of up to EUR 35 million or 7% of global annual turnover for the most serious violations. SMEs face existential risk from non-compliance.

How AI Comply Helps

  • Plain-language guidance — Translates complex legal requirements into clear, actionable checklists and step-by-step wizards that non-lawyers can follow.
  • Automatic risk classification — Answer a few questions about your AI system and the platform determines the correct risk tier, Annex III category, and applicable articles.
  • Obligation mapping — Once classified, the platform shows exactly which of the 7 high-risk obligations (Art. 9-15) apply and tracks compliance status for each.
  • Automated testing — Run 29 automated compliance tests covering risk management, data governance, transparency, human oversight, accuracy, and documentation.
  • Remediation guidance — Every failed test comes with specific, actionable remediation steps and effort estimates so teams know exactly what to fix.
  • Audit-ready evidence — Generates timestamped compliance reports with test results, findings, and remediation records that can be presented to regulators.
Section 03
How We Could Make It Better
The current platform is a strong foundation. Here are high-impact improvements that would significantly increase value for SME users.
  1. Live document scanning & gap analysis — Let users upload their existing documentation (risk assessments, data sheets, technical docs) and automatically identify gaps against EU AI Act requirements. Currently, compliance status is manually tracked; AI-powered document analysis would save hours of manual review per system.
  2. Continuous monitoring & alerting — Move from point-in-time reports to continuous compliance monitoring. Watch for model drift, logging failures, or documentation expiry and send alerts before they become audit findings. This turns AI Comply from a "check once" tool into an always-on compliance co-pilot.
  3. Multi-tenant team collaboration — Add role-based access (Compliance Officer, Developer, Auditor, Executive), assignment workflows, and comment threads on findings. SMEs typically have 3-10 people involved in AI compliance across legal, engineering, and management.
  4. Conformity assessment workflow — Build the full Article 43 conformity assessment pipeline: internal control procedures, third-party assessment routing, CE marking preparation, and EU database registration (Art. 49). This is the most complex compliance requirement and no affordable tool supports it today.
  5. Regulatory change tracking — The EU AI Act has delegated acts, implementing acts, and harmonised standards still being developed. Auto-track regulatory updates and surface which of your systems are affected by new requirements or interpretive guidance.
  6. API-first architecture & integrations — Expose a REST API so that CI/CD pipelines can run compliance checks on every deployment. Integrate with existing tools: Jira (finding tracking), Confluence (documentation), GitHub (code-level checks), Slack (alerts).
  7. Benchmarking & industry comparisons — Show anonymised compliance benchmarks so SMEs can see how they compare to peers in their industry. This motivates action and helps prioritise the most commonly-failed requirements.
  8. Multi-regulation support — Expand beyond the EU AI Act to cover GDPR AI provisions, the AI Liability Directive, sector-specific rules (MDR for medical AI, MiFID for financial AI), and upcoming regulations in the UK, US, and Canada.
